Applications of Electric Fields

A parallel plate capacitor is used to generate a (nearly) uniform electric field.

  • The field is non-uniform near the edeges (“edge effects”).

A conductor placed in an electric field will become polarized in such a way that the net electric field inside the conductor will be zero.

  • This effect is called shielding.
  • Equipment that is sensitive to electric fields is often built inside a metal frame.

An electrical discharge (e.g. lightning) occurs when the field becomes strong enough to ionize air molecules.

  • The force on the negative electrons acts in the opposite direction to the force on the positive nuclei.
  • The net force on a neutral molecule in a uniform field is zero.
  • If the field gets strong enough, some electrons are pulled loose from the molecule.
  • These electons accelarate toward the positve plate while the now positive molecules accelerate toward the negative plate.
  • The charge on the plates is reduced whenever an oppositely-charged particle reaches the plate.
  • Light is emitted when free electrons recombine with ionized molecules.
  • For a non-uniform field, the discharge is most likely to begin where the electric field is strongest.
  • This is how lightning rods protect buildings and equipment from lightning strikes.
