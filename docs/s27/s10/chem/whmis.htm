WHMIS / Lab Safety

WHMIS 1988

WHMIS stands for Workplace Hazardous Materials Information System.

  • WHMIS is the Canadian government system for identifying hazards in the workplace.
  • WHMIS requires hazardous material to be properly labelled and for the label to include a warning and pictogram.

  • Your textbook illustrates the pictograms for WHMIS 1988, the year WHMIS was first standardized.

WHMIS 2015 & GHS

The Global Harmonized System (GHS) is an international agreement to use the same hazard pictograms in different countries.

  • WHMIS was revised in 2015 to be compatible with the GHS.
  • WHMIS 2015 pictograms are easily recognized by the red diamond outline.

  • WHMIS 2015 pictograms are shown on the WHMIS website.

Safety Data Sheets

Another requirement is that each hazardous material have an available Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS or SDS).

Every SDS has a number of requirements including:

  • Name & Formula
  • Physical Properties
  • Stability & Reactivity
  • Potential Health Effects
  • Handling & Storage
  • Disposal
