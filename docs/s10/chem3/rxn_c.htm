Lesson Notes

Combustion

Oxidation

Reactions with oxygen (\rm{O_2}) can be classified as oxidation reactions.

  • An example of oxidation could be the rusting of iron:

    \rm{Fe + O_2 \rightarrow Fe_2 O_3}

  • This reaction is also a formation.
  • Rusting of iron is a very slow oxidation.
  • A rapid, exothermic oxidation is called combustion.
  • Combustion reactions release heat and usually produce a visible flame.

Hydrocarbon Combustion

Hydrocarbons are molecular compounds made from carbon and hydrogen, such as methane (CH4), ethane (C2H6), and propane (C3H8).

  • Hydrocarbons burn (combust) in oxygen to produce water and carbon dioxide:

    \rm{C_3H_8 + O_2 \rightarrow H_2O + CO_2}

  • Reactions like this are called hydrocarbon combustion.
  • Sometimes carbon monoxide can be produced instead of, or in addition to carbon dioxide. This is an incomplete combustion.
  • Hydrocarbon combustion is an exothermic reaction that releases heat and light.
  • Balance carbon first, then hydrogen, and oxygen last.

    \rm{C_3H_8 + O_2 \rightarrow H_2O + 3\ CO_2}

    \rm{C_3H_8 + O_2 \rightarrow 4\ H_2O + 3\ CO_2}

    \rm{C_3H_8 + 5\ O_2 \rightarrow 4\ H_2O + 3\ CO_2}

  • When counting oxygen atoms, remember to total the oxygens in the water molecules and those in the carbon dioxide.
  • When the number of oxygens on one side is even and on the other side is odd, changing the coefficient of \rm O_2 will not work because this will always add an even number of oxygens!
  • When this happens, double all of the other coefficients to make the number of atoms even on both sides.

\rm C_4H_{10} + O_2 \rightarrow H_2O + CO_2

\rm C_4H_{10} + O_2 \rightarrow H_2O + 4\ CO_2

\rm C_4H_{10} + O_2 \rightarrow 5\ H_2O + 4\ CO_2

\rm 2\ C_4H_{10} + O_2 \rightarrow 10\ H_2O + 8\ CO_2

\rm 2\ C_4H_{10} + 13\ O_2 \rightarrow 10\ H_2O + 8\ CO_2

Carbohydrates

  • Compounds containing carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen atoms are called carbohydrates.
  • Carbohydrates include sugars and alcohols.
  • Balance the oxidation/combustion of carbohydrates the same way you do for hydrocarbons.
  • The reaction of glucose with oxygen will be very important in the Biology Unit!

Review

  1. Describe oxidation and combustion reactions.

    2. Oxidation is the reaction of an element or compound with oxygen to produce oxides. Rapid, exothermic oxidation reactions are called combustion. Slower oxidation reactions include the rusting of iron or other metals.

  2. Describe hydrocarbon combustion.

    3. Combustion of hydrocarbons (compounds of carbon, hydrogen, and sometimes oxygen) generally produce water and carbon dioxide as products. “Incomplete” hydrocarbon combustion may produce carbon monoxide instead of or in addition to carbon dioxide.
