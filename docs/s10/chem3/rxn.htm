Chemical Reactions

Chemical Reactions

A chemical reaction is a rearrangement of chemical bonds to produce new substances.

  • For example when we run electricity through liquid water, it decomposes into hydrogen and oxygen gases:

    \rm H_2O_{(l)} \rightarrow H_{2(g)} + O_{2(g)}

  • The left side of the reaction equation describes the reactant(s).
  • The right side of the reaction equation describes the product(s).
  • The bracketed subscripts identify the phase: solid, liquid, gas, or aqueous.
  • This reaction is unbalanced because the number of oxygen atoms is different between the left and right sides.
  • Since we need two oxygen atoms to make O2, we must start with two water molecules.

    \rm 2\ H_2O_{(l)} \rightarrow H_{2(g)} + O_{2(g)}

  • Now we have more hydrogen atoms on the left side (4) so we need to double the hydrogen gas.

    \rm 2\ H_2O_{(l)} \rightarrow 2\ H_{2(g)} + O_{2(g)}

  • This reaction equation is balanced because the number of atoms of each element is the same on both sides.
  • The total mass of the products equals the total mass of the reactants. This is called the Law of Conservation of Mass.

Exothermic & Endothermic

Because the products of a chemical reaction are different than the reactants, the chemical energy.

  • When the products have less chemical energy than the reactants, then energy released often takes the form of thermal energy and possibly light.
  • Reactions that release energy are called exothermic.
  • Exothermic reactions can often be identified by an increase in temperature.
  • When the products have more chemical energy than the reactants, then energy is often taken from the surroundings.
  • Reactions that absorb energy are called endothermic.
  • Endothermic reactions can often be identified by a decrease in temperature.

Review

  1. Define and explain what happens during a chemical reaction.

    2. Chemical bonds (ionic or covalent) are broken and new bonds are formed instead. The result is a change in the type of substance(s) present.

  2. Define:
    1. reactant

      2. A substance that is present before the reaction that will be changed into something else

    2. product

      3. A substance that is produced by the chemical reaction

  3. List three observations that would constitute evidence that a chemical reaction has occurred.

    4. Change in temperature; change of colour; production of a gas; production of a precipitate

    1. State the Law of Conservation of Mass.

      2. Total mass remains unchanged during a chemical reaction; the total mass of the products is exactly the same as the total mass of the reactants.

      Mass must be conserved because a chemical reaction is a re-arrangement of the bonds between atoms. The total number of atoms of each element remains unchanged.

      These laws are not applicable to nuclear and other sub-atomic reactions!

    2. State the First Law of Thermodynamics, also called Law of Energy Conservation.

      3. Energy can be transformed from one form to another but the total energy must always remain constant

    3. Define an endothermic and an exothermic reaction.

      4. Reactions that convert thermal energy (heat) into chemical energy are endothermic; they cause a temperature drop. Exothermic reactions transform chemical energy into thermal energy and result in a temperature rise.

  5. What is the source of energy that is released in an exothermic reaction? What absorbs the energy that is released? In other words, where does the energy come from and where does it go?

    6. The source of the energy is the chemical energy of the reactants. The products of an exothermic Reaction have less energy than the reactants. The difference is released into the surroundings as thermal energy (heat), light, and/or sound.

  6. What is the source of energy that is absorbed in an endothermic reaction?

    7. The source of energy is the surroundings. Thermal energy is taken from the surroundings (causing the temperature to drop) and added to the reactants’ chemical energy. The products will have more chemical energy than the reactants.

  7. A student claims that the reaction of butane gas and oxygen must be endothermic since a spark is required to ignite butane lighter. Do you agree or disagree? Explain.

    8. It does take a bit of energy to start the reaction, since the existing chemical bonds in the reactants Need to be broken. However, the amount of energy released when the products are formed is much greater. The reaction is classified as exothermic.
