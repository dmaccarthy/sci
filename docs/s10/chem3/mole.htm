Molar Mass

How much iron and hydrogen phosphate do I need to react to produce iron(II) phosphate?

\rm{3\ Fe + 2\ H_3PO_4 \rightarrow Fe_3(PO_4)_2 + 3\ H_2}

By about 1915, scientists were able to determine the mass of each proton or neutron is about \rm 1.67\times 10^{-24}\ g.

  • The reciprocal of this mass is called Avagadro's number:

    N_A = 6.02\times 10^{23}

  • Avagadro's number is the number of protons or neutrons needed to make a gram.
  • A mole is a quantity equal to Avagadro's number of atoms or molecules.
  • One mole (Avagadro's number) of hydrogen-1 atoms has a mass of 1.00 gram.
  • One mole of hydrogen-2 atoms has a mass of 2.00 grams because there are twice as many particles.
  • One mole of hydrogen atoms in nature has a mass of 1.01 grams, because most hydrogen atoms are H-1.
  • This is the molar mass shown on the periodic table.
  • Hydrogen molecules (\rm{H_2}) have a molar mass of 2.02 grams.

Calculate the mass of 2.50 moles of iron(II) phosphate.

\rm{2.50\ mol\times 357.49\ g/mol = 894\ g}

nM = m

n = {m\over M}

  • n is the number of moles of a substance.
  • M is the molar mass (i.e. the mass for 1 mole).
  • m is the total mass (i.e. the mass for n moles).

Review

  1. How many atoms of hydrogen-1 does it take to have a total mass of 1.00 gram? What do we call this number?

    2. 1.00 g of hydrogen-1 would contain 6.02 × 1023 atoms. This number is called Avogadro’s number. This quantity of atoms is called one mole.

    1. Why is it that the atomic molar mass of hydrogen is not exactly 1.00 g/ mol?

      2. Some naturally occurring atoms of hydrogen are of the isotope hydrogen-2. The average molar mass for hydrogen atoms is slightly larger than 1.00 g/mol.

    2. Why is the molecular molar mass of hydrogen twice as large as its atomic molar mass?

      3. Hydrogen occurs at SATP as a diatomic molecule, H2. The hydrogen molecule has a mass equal to two hydrogen atoms.
