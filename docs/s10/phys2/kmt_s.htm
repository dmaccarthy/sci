Heat as a Fluid?

Phlogiston: A fluid that leaves a substance when it burns.

  • This idea was discarded as some materials increase in mass when they burn.

Caloric: A massless fluid that flows from hot to cold.

  • Count Rumford: Observed that friction (e.g. drilling cannon bores) produced heat.
  • Julius Meyer: Observed that in warm climates, the body used less oxygen. This indicates that the body needs less energy.
  • James Joule: Conducted an experiment in which gravitational potential energy was used to stir water.

  • The water's temperature was observed to increase.
  • It takes 4.19 joules (1 calorie) of energy to raise the temperature of each gram of water by 1°C.
  • Joule concluded that heat is related to energy!

Kinetic Molecular Theory

  • The atoms/molecules of every substance are in constant motion.
  • Temperature is a measure of the average kinetic energy of these moving particles.
  • The temperature at which the particles have no kinetic energy at all is called absolute zero.
  • Absolute zero or 0 Kelvin = –273.15°C.
  • When objects at different temeratures are in contact, collisions between particles transfer energy from the hotter particles to the colder ones.
  • Pressure is the force of the moving particles colliding with their container. Hotter temperatures produce higher pressures.

Phases of Matter

Substances are in the gas phase (or state) when the molecules are far apart.

  • Molecules in gases travel long distances between collisions.
  • Gases expand to fill their container.
  • Gases exert more pressure at higher temperatures.

In the liquid phase, attractive forces between the molecules keep them in contact with neighbouring molecules.

  • The forces are not strong enough to keep the individual molecules in place.
  • Liquids flow to take on the shape of their container.
  • Liquids maintain a fixed volume when their temperature is constant.
  • When heated, liquids usually expand due to the extra kinetic energy each molecule has.

In the solid phase, attractive forces keep the molecules from flowing.

  • Individual molecules vibrate in place.
  • Solids maintain a fixed shape as well as volume.
  • Like liquids, solids expand when heated as the molecules vibrate faster.

Second Law of Thermodynamics

It is not possible to extract all of the heat from a system and convert it entirely to other forms of energy.

Heat flows from higher to lower temperature.

Every energy transformation has an efficiency less than 100%.

The “entropy” of any closed system can never decrease.
