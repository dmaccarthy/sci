Position & Displacement

Mechanics

The branch of Physics that studies motion is called mechanics.

The main sub-branches of mechanics are:

  • Kinematics: Describes the motion of an object.
  • Dynamics: Explains the motion of an object.

Position

The first step in describing an object’s motion is to describe its position; where is it?

Typically, we start by choosing a convenient location as the origin.

  • The origin is assigned a coordinate of zero.
  • The choice of origin is completely arbitrary.
  • Points along the coordinate axis are represented by the distance from the origin.
  • Positive coordinates are used in one direction and negative coordinates in the other.
  • For motion in a straight line, only one coordinate axis is needed. This is called one dimensional (1D) motion.
  • Motion on a sports field would be two dimensional (2D) motion because it requires two coordinate axis.

Displacement

A moving object will have a position that is changing over time.

The change in an object’s position between two times is called its displacement:

\Delta\vec{\bf d} = \vec{\bf d}_f - \vec{\bf d}_i

  • The subscripts i and f stand for initial and final.
  • The symbol \Delta is the Greek letter “Delta”, which stands for difference (change).

\Delta\vec{\bf d} = \rm{(+7.0\ m) - (-5.0\ m) = +12.0\ m}

The bike undergoes a displacement of \rm{+12.0\ m} as it moves from an initial position of \rm{-5.0\ m} to a final position of \rm{+7.0\ m}.

Displacement is illustrated on a vector diagram by an arrow pointing from the object’s initial position to its final position.

  • Saying \Delta\vec{\bf d} = \rm{+12.0\ m} means that the arrow points in the positive direction and is 12.0 metres long.

Vectors & Scalars

Quantities that involve a direction, such as displacement, are called vectors.

  • When we say the displacement is \Delta\vec{\bf d} = \rm{+12.0\ m}, this means that the magnitude (size) of the vector is 12.0 metres and the direction is positive.
  • Other vectors include velocity (\vec{\bf v}) and force (\vec{\bf F}).
  • Vector variables are written with an arrow above the symbol, or printed in bold type.

Quantities that do not involve a direction are called scalars.

  • Scalars include time (t), mass (m), and energy (E).
  • Scalar variables are written without an arrow, or printed in italics.

Distance

The distance that an object moves is a scalar; it does not involve a direction.

  • Distance is measured along the path (or trajectory) that the object actually follows.
  • Displacement is measured in a straight line between the initial and final positions, regardless of the object’s trajectory.
  • If the object follows an indirect route, its distance will be more than the magnitude of its displacement.

Practice

Review

  1. Define scalar and a vector.
    2. A vector is a quantity that involves a direction, such as velocity or force. A scalar is a quantity that does not have a direction, such as time or mass.
  2. How do we measure an object’s position in physics? In your answer, include definitions for the terms: origin and coordinate.
    3. A coordinate is a measurement that represents an object’s position or location. It is often just a distance from an arbitrary point called the origin. The origin itself has a coordinate of zero. Positions on one side of the origin are assigned positive coordinates and the other side is assigned negative coordinates.
  3. Define and give the formula for displacement.
    4. Displacement (\Delta\vec{\bf d}) is the difference or change in an object’s position at two different times.

    \Delta\vec{\bf d} = \vec{\bf d}_f - \vec{\bf d}_i

    The symbol \vec{\bf d}_i is the initial position and \vec{\bf d}_f is the final position.
  4. Explain how displacement and distance are different.
    5. Distance is a scalar that measures how far an object has travelled along its trajectory (path). Displacement is a vector that compares where the object ends up to where it starts. Displacement is always measured along the most direct path (a straight line) even if the object does not follow a straight trajectory.
