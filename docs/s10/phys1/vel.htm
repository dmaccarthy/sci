Lesson Notes

Velocity & Speed

Velocity

We learned in the last lesson that displacement measures the change in an object’s position.

Velocity measures how quickly the position changes, or the rate of displacement:

\vec{\bf v} = {\Delta\vec{\bf d}\over \Delta t}

  • Velocity, like displacement, is a vector.
  • The SI unit of velocity is metres per second.
  • The conversion factor to or from km/h is

    \rm{1\ m/s = 3.6\ km/h}

Motion Graph

\vec{\bf v} = {\Delta\vec{\bf d}\over \Delta t}

\vec{\bf v} = \rm{(+7.0\ m) - (-5.0\ m)\over 4.0\ s - 0.0\ s}

\begin{align}&= \rm{{+12.0\ m \over 4.0\ s}}\\ &= \rm{+3.0\ m/s}\end{align}

  • Since \Delta\vec{\bf d} is the “rise” of the graph and \Delta t is the “run”, velocity is the slope of the line.

When the graph is a straight line, the motion is classified as uniform motion.

  • The graph has a constant slope.
  • The velocity is constant.
  • The position changes by the same amount in equal amounts of time.

When the graph is curved, the motion is classified as accelerated motion.

  • The slope of the graph is not constant.
  • The velocity is changing.
  • The position changes by the different amounts in equal amounts of time.

Speed

Speed is the rate of distance travelled:

v = {\Delta d\over \Delta t}

  • Remember that distance is measured along the path of the motion, while displacement is measured in a straight line.
  • Speed is a scalar (no direction) while velocity is a vector.

Example 1

What will be the displacement of a cyclist who rides with a velocity of 27.0 km/h [south] for one minute?

Identify the variables and convert to standard SI units if necessary:

  • \vec{\bf v} = \rm{27.0\ km/h\ [S] = 7.50\ m/s\ [S]}
  • \Delta t =\ \rm{1.00\ min = 60.0\ s}
  • \Delta\vec{\bf d} =\ ?

Select the relevant relationship (equation):

\vec{\bf v} = {\Delta \vec{\bf d}\over \Delta t}

Rearrange the equation to isolate the unknown:

\vec{\bf v}{\color{red}{\Delta t}} = \frac{\Delta \vec{\bf d}}{\Delta t} {\color{red}{\Delta t}}

\vec{\bf v}\Delta t = \Delta \vec{\bf d}

Evaluate, including units:

\Delta \vec{\bf d} = \rm{{7.50\ m/s\ [S] \cdot 60.0\ s} = 450\ m\ [S]}

  • Is the answer reasonable?

Example 2

An athlete can maintain an average speed of 7.80 m/s during an 800-metre race. How long will it take the runner to finish the race?

Identify the variables:

  • v = \rm{7.80\ m/s}
  • \Delta d = \rm{800\ m}
  • \Delta t =\ ?

Select the relevant relationship (equation):

v = {\Delta d\over \Delta t}

Rearrange the equation to isolate the unknown:

v{\color{red}{\Delta t}} = \frac{\Delta d}{\Delta t} {\color{red}{\Delta t}}

v\Delta t = \Delta d

\frac{v\Delta t}{\color{red}{v}} = \frac{\Delta d}{\color{red}{v}}

\Delta t = \frac{\Delta d}{v}

Evaluate, including units:

\Delta t = \rm{{800\ m \over 7.80\ m/s} = 103\ s}

  • Always use standard SI units (except when the units cancel).
  • Is the answer reasonable?

Review

    1. Define and give the formula for velocity.

      2. Velocity is the rate at which displacement occurs (the displacement per second):

      \vec{\bf v} = \frac{\Delta\vec{\bf d}}{\Delta t}

    2. Explain the connection between the velocity equation and the slope equation. Why is this important?

      3. When we graph the position (\vec{\bf d}) against the time (t), the displacement will be the rise of the graph and the time interval will be the run, so the velocity will equal the slope of the graph.

  2. Explain how speed is different from velocity.

    3. Speed is the rate at which distance (not displacement) increases, so speed is a scalar while velocity is a vector.

  3. What is the SI unit of velocity and speed? How do we convert to or from km/h?

    4. The SI unit is m/s. You can divide a measurement in km/h by 3.6 to get m/s and multiply m/s by 3.6 to get km/h.

  4. Explain how to solve motion problems where the velocity (or speed) is already known and you must calculate either the time or displacement (or distance)?

    5. Use algebra to rearrange the velcity formula to solve for displacement or time:

    \Delta\vec{\bf d} = \vec{\bf v} \Delta t

    \Delta t = \frac{\Delta\vec{\bf d}}{\vec{\bf v}}

  5. Define the following types of motion and explain how to determine from a graph what type of motion is occurring: (a) uniform motion, (b) accelerated motion.

    6. Uniform motion means the velocity is constant and the graph will be a straight line (constant slope). Accelerated motion means the velocity is changing so the graph will be curved.
