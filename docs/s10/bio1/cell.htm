Characteristics of Life

  1. Need energy: eat, photosynthesis.
  2. Produce wastes.
  3. Grow.
  4. Reproduce.
  5. Respond: to the environment.

Abiogenesis

Aristotle observed frogs in mud, and mice in wheat husks.

  • He hypothesized the spontaneous generation of these animals from non-living material.
  • Spontaneous generation is also called abiogenesis:
    • genesis = creation
    • bio = life
    • a = not, opposite
  • Redi (1668) used a controlled experiment with flies to disprove abiogenesis.
  • The experiment tested whether maggots could arise from rotting meat by covering one jar of meat and leaving the other uncovered.

  • Maggots developed in the open jars only meat but not in the covered jars.
  • This shows that something from outside (flies!) produced the maggots, and covering the jar prevented this.

Biogenesis

Living organisms can only arise from other living organisms.

  • The theory of biogenesis was proposed by Virchow (1858).
  • Pasteur (1850) studied fermentation and sour milk caused by microorganisms.
  • He showed that you could slow the spoiling of milk by preventing microorganisms from entering, and by killing exiting microbes with heat.

Cell Theory

Schleiden, Schwann, & Virchow proposed the basics of cell theory...

  1. All organisms are composed of one or more cells.
  2. The cell is the smallest functional unit of life. (Just like atoms are the smallest unit of matter in chemistry)
  3. All cells are produced from other cells.

Review

  1. Define abiogenesis and biogenesis.

    2. Abiogenesis is the idea that life can arise from non-living matter. Biogenesis says that life can only arise from living things.

  2. Describe Redi’s experiment including a diagram. State what the manipulated, responding, and controlled variables were. What was his conclusion?

    3. Place meat (Controlled) in covered and uncovered jars (Manipulated).

    Maggots (Responding) appear only in the uncovered jar.

    Conclusion: Maggots did not arise from the meat but from the flies.

  3. Describe the contributions to cell theory of:
    1. Robert Hooke

      2. Saw cork cells in a primitive microscope and coined the term “cell”.

    2. Anton von Leeuwenhoek

      3. Improved the microscope design and saw single-celled organisms in his microscope.

    3. Louis Pasteur

      4. Demonstrated that microorganisms caused food spoilage but did not arise from the food itself.

    4. Schleiden & Schwann

      5. Concluded that all plant and animal tissues are composed of cells.

    5. Rudolf Virchow

      6. Concluded that cells are created from pre-existing cells.

  4. State the three basic principles of cell theory.
    1. All organisms (life) are composed of one or more cells.
    2. The cell is the smallest functional unit of life.
    3. All cells are produced from other cells.
