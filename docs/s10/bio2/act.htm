Active Transport

Active Transport

Active transport refers to the movement of materials through a membrane in a way that requires the cell to expend energy.

  • Active transport often occurs when the cell needs to move materials against the concentration gradient.
  • For example, plants roots need to absorb minerals even if the concentration in the soil is lower than the concentration already inside the cell.
  • Carrier proteins can use active transport to move some molecules through the membrane.

Endocytosis & Exocytosis

  • Endocytosis is a form of active transport where material enters the cell.
  • The membrance forms a bulge in which the material settles.
  • The bulge grows larger and the neck pinches together.
  • The portion of the membrane enclosing the material separates from the cell membrane and becomes a new vesicle inside the cell.
  • Phagocytosis refers to endocytosis where the material is solid.
  • Pinocytosis refers to endocytosis where the material is liquid.
  • Exocytosis is the reverse of endocytosis
  • A vesicle containing waste or other material to be removed from the cell merges with the cell membrane and opens to expel the material.
