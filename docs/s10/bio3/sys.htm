Plant Control Systems

Tropisms

A plant’s response to a stimulus is called a tropism:

  • Phototropism: Response to light.
  • Gravitropism: Response to gravity.
  • Nastic Response: Response to touch (e.g. Venus fly trap).

Phototropism

A plant benefits by growing towards the Sun (or an artificial light source) to maximize photosynthesis.

  • Plants cannot move, but different parts of the plant can grow at different rates.
  • Growing cells in the stem contain a hormone called auxin that stimulates growth.
  • Auxin is moved by active transport from the side of the stem more exposed to light, to the darker side.
  • Cells on the darker side grow faster due to the greater concentration of auxin.
  • This differential growth cause the plant to bend toward the light source.

Gravitropism

Gravitropism is a plant’s growth response to the force of gravity.

  • Negative gravitropism means growth in the opposite direction as gravity.
  • This allows the stem to grow upward.

  • Positive gravitropism means growth in the same direction as gravity.
  • This allows roots to grow downward.

Review

  1. Define:
    1. tropism

      2. A plant’s response to stimuli

    2. phototropism

      3. A plant’s response to light; i.e. it will grow so that its leaves are pointed toward light

    3. positive gravitropism

      4. Growth of the root in the same direction as gravity (down)

    4. negative gravitropism

      5. Growth of the stem in the opposite direction as gravity (up)

    5. nastic response

      6. Response to touch (as in carnivorous plants)
