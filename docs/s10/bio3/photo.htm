Photosynthesis & Cellular Respiration

Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is a chemical reaction that occurs in the chloroplasts of plant cells (and in a few other organisms).

  • Photosynthnesis uses energy from sunlight to produce glucose:

    \rm{6\ CO_2 + 6\ H_2O \rightarrow C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6\ O_2}

  • For photosynthesis to occur, the chloroplast must have access to water, carbon dioxide, and light.
  • Chloroplasts contain a green pigment called chlorophyll that absorbs energy from sunlight and initiates the reaction.
  • The chemical reaction is complicated and involves several steps.
  • In the light dependent portion of the reaction, light energy is converted to ATP.
  • In the light independent portion of the reaction, the ATP energy is used to produce glucose.
  • All life relies on photosynthesis for energy.
  • Organisms that can meet their own energy needs using photosynthesis are called autotrophic.
  • Organisms that cannot perform photosynthesis are called heterotrophic. Heterotrophs must obtain energy by consuming other organisms.

Cellular Respiration

When cells require energy, they break down glucose in a process called cellular respiration.

  • The chemical reaction for cellular respiration is the reverse of photosynthesis:

    \rm C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6\ O_2 \rightarrow 6\ CO_2 + 6\ H_2O

  • The energy released by this reaction is used to create ATP, which the cell then uses for its energy requirements.
  • As the reaction shows, cells require oxygen as well as glucose.

Review

  1. In what kinds of organisms does photosynthesis occur?

    2. Plants and some single-celled organisms

  2. What is the chemical equation for photosynthesis?

    3. \rm{6\ CO_2 + 6\ H_2O \rightarrow C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6\ O_2}

    [carbon dioxide + water → glucose + oxygen]

  3. Where in the cell (i.e. in which organelle) does photosynthesis occur?

    4. Chloroplast

  4. Why is photosynthesis so important?

    5. This allows the plant to store energy from sunlight which it needs to survive.

  5. What is cellular respiration? In which organelle does it occur?

    6. This is the opposite of photosynthesis. The glucose is reacted to release the energy in the form of a chemical ATP. It occurs it the mitochondria.

  6. What is the chemical equation for cellular respiration?

    7. \rm{C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6\ O_2 \rightarrow 6\ CO_2 + 6\ H_2O}

  7. How is cellular respiration similar to/different than combustion?

    8. The reaction is very similar but it occurs much more slowly.

  8. What is ATP? What is its connection to photosynthesis and cellular respiration?

    9. It is a chemical that the cell uses to store energy for immediate use.
