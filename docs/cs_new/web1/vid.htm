Adding Video

HTML5 introduced a <video> tag for embedding video files in web pages. Because different browsers may support different video formats, you can list multiple versions of the video in the <source> tags which are children of the <video> tag.

In the example below, the HTML embeds a video resized to 640 by 360 pixels with user controls (play, pause, etc.) displayed. Two versions of the video are provided for the browser to choose from: an MP4 and an OGV format.

<video width="640" height="360" controls="1">
  <source src="https://dmaccarthy.github.io/sci/media/movie.mp4" type="video/mp4">
  <source src="https://dmaccarthy.github.io/sci/media/movie.ogv" type="video/ogg">
</video>

YouTube

Rather than upload videos to your own web server, many web pages rely on video hosting services such as YouTube to provide embedded video. By including videos in this manner, you do not need to worry about the video format. To embed a YouTube video in your web page, visit the video’s YouTube page and obtain the HTML code from the Share/Embed section.

<iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/Vd5duzNtBuI"
    width="640" height="360" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe>

Depending on your web browser settings, you may or may not be able to preview the example as a “data blob”. Instead, you can download the example and then open the downloaded file with your web browser. Important: Never open downloaded files that do not come from a trusted source!
