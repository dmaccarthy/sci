In this chapter, you will learn how to describe motion using concepts like displacement, velocity, and acceleration, and how to solve problems involving uniform accelerated motion.

  • Lesson 1: Position & Displacement
  • Lesson 2: Velocity & Speed
  • Lesson 3: Motion Graphs
  • Lesson 4: Acceleration
  • Lesson 5: Uniform Accelerated Motion

Chapter 6: Work & Energy

This chapter introduces the Law of Energy Conservation and the Work-Energy Theorem as additional methods for solving motion problems.

  • Lesson 1: Mechanical Energy
  • Lesson 2: Energy Conservation
  • Lesson 3: Non-Isolated Systems
  • Lesson 4: Energy Transformations (Work)
  • Lesson 5: Work-Energy Theorem
  • Lesson 6: Power & Efficiency

Chapter 3: Newtons Law’s

In this chapter you will learn how forces affect the motion of an object according to Newton’s Laws.

  • Lesson 1: Forces & Inertia
  • Lesson 2: Free Body Diagrams
  • Lesson 3: Newton’s 3rd Law
  • Lesson 4: Mass & Weight
  • Lesson 5: Contact Forces

Chapter 7: Simple Harmonic Motion

In this chapter, you will learn about oscillatory (“back and forth”) motion such as a simple pendulum. Before you begin with SHM, you will learn how to analyze experimental data when it is non-linear:

  • Lesson 1: Regression Analysis
  • Lesson 2: Transforming Power Data
  • Lesson 3: Equilibrium & Oscillations
  • Lesson 4: Simple Harmonic Motion
  • Lesson 5: Period of SHM
  • Lesson 6: Simple Pendulum
  • Lesson 7: Energy of SHM
  • Lesson 8: Mechanical Resonance
  • Chapter Review
  • Data Analysis Review

Chapter 8: Mechanical Waves

In this chapter, you will learn about the behaviour of waves such as sound, surface waves on water, and waves in some musical instruments:

  • Lesson 1: Harmonic Waves
  • Lesson 2: Doppler Effect
  • Lesson 3: Interference & Beats
  • Lesson 4: Standing Waves
  • Chapter Review

Chapter 5: Circular & Planetary Motion

In this chapter you will learn about motion in a circle as well as about Kepler’s Law of Planetary Motion.

  • Lesson 1: Uniform Circular Motion
  • Lesson 2: Centripetal Acceleration
  • Lesson 3: Apparent Weight
  • Lesson 4: Planetary Motion (Kepler’s Laws)
  • Chapter Review

Chapter 4: Universal Gravitation

In this chapter you will learn that gravity is a universal force that acts between all masses and over long distances:

  • Lesson 1: Universal Gravitation
  • Lesson 2: Cavendish Experiment
  • Lesson 3: Gravitational Fields
  • Lesson 4: Circular Orbital Motion
  • Chapter Review

In this chapter, you will learn how to describe describe 2D vectors in polar and Cartesian form, how to perform arithmetic and algebra on 2D vectors, and how to solve projectile motion problems.
