Lesson Notes

SI Units

What are Units?

A unit is a standard of comparison for measurements.

  • To measure the length of an object, we compare its length to the markings on a ruler.

  • For the measurement to make sense to others, we need to use a standardized distance between markings such as centimetres or inches.
  • The definition of each unit is arbitrary.

Fundamental Units

Scientists have agreed that the official unit for measuring distances will be the metre, {\mathrm m}.

  • A metre is officially defined as a certain number of wavelengths of a certain colour of light.

The Système Internationale uses seven fundamental units (also called base units) upon which all other units are based.

Derived Units

Fundamental units can be combined to make new units. These are known as derived units.

  • To measure velocity, we use \mathrm{m/s}.
  • To measure density, we use \mathrm{kg/m^3}.

Sometimes derived units are given their own name and symbol.

  • The SI unit of force is called the newton:

    \mathrm{N = kg \cdot {m\over s^2}}

  • Other derived units with names include: joule, watt, pascal, volt.

SI Prefixes

When an SI unit is too small to be practical, we can use prefixes to create larger units.

  • SI prefixes always change the size of units based on powers of 10.
  • This is much simpler than having to remember 12 inches in a foot, 3 feet in a yard, and 1760 yards in a mile.

  • The exception to this is kilograms!

Unit Conversions

The factor-label method of unit conversion can be used whenever we want to replace a unit by a different but equivalent unit...

Example 1

Convert \mathrm{25.0\ \mu m} to \mathrm m.

We know that \mathrm{1\ \mu m = 10^{-6}\ m}.

Write the measurement in scientific notation, and multiply the measurement by a fraction with the two equivalent measurements as the numerator and denominator...

\mathrm{25.0\ \mu m = 2.50\times 10^1\ \mu m\ \color{red}{\frac{10^{-6}\ m}{1\ \mu m}}}

  • Decide the “top” and “bottom” so that the original unit cancels.

\mathrm{25.0\ \mu m} = \mathrm{2.50\times 10^1\ \bcancel{\mu m}\ \color{red}{\frac{10^{-6}\ m}{1\ \bcancel{\mu m}}}}

= \mathrm{2.50\times 10^{-5}\ m}

Example 2

Convert \mathrm{5.00\times 10^{-7}\ s} to \mathrm {ns}.

\mathrm{1\ ns = 10^{-9}\ s}...

\mathrm{5.00\times 10^{-7}\ s} = \mathrm{5.00\times 10^{-7}\ \bcancel{s}\ \color{red}{\frac{1\ ns}{10^{-9}\ \bcancel{s}}}}

= \mathrm{5.00\times 10^2\ ns}

Example 3

Convert \mathrm{1.50\ mW/cm^2} to \mathrm{W/m^2}.

\mathrm{1\ mW = 10^{-3}\ W} and \mathrm{1\ cm = 10^{-2}\ m}...

\mathrm{1.50\ \frac{mW}{cm^2}} = \mathrm{1.50\ \frac{\bcancel{mW}}{\bcancel{cm^2}} \color{red}{\frac{10^{-3}\ W}{1\ \bcancel{mW}}} \color{blue}{\left(\frac{1\ \bcancel{cm}}{10^{-2}\ m}\right)^2}}

= \mathrm{1.50\ \frac{10^{-3}\ W}{10^{-4}\ m^2}}

= \mathrm{15.0\ W/m^2}

  • When a unit is squared or cubed, you must square or cube the factor-label fraction too!

Example 4

Convert \mathrm{72.0\ km/h} to \mathrm{m/s}.

\mathrm{1\ km = 10^3\ m} and \mathrm{1\ h = 3600\ s}...

\mathrm{72.0\frac{km}{h}} = \mathrm{72.0\frac{\bcancel{km}}{\bcancel{h}}\ \color{red}{\frac{10^3\ m}{1\ \bcancel{km}}} \color{blue}{\frac{\bcancel{h}}{3600\ s}}}

= \mathrm{20.0\ m/s}

  • A shortcut for converting km/h to m/s is to divide by 3.6.

Practice

  1. Convert these measurements to standard SI units.
    1. 32.5 grams
    2. 625 μs
    3. 6.40 cm3
    4. 80.0 km/h

    \mathrm{32.5\ g = 3.25\times 10^1\ \bcancel{g}\ \color{blue}{\frac{1\ kg}{10^3\ \bcancel{g}}} = 3.25\times 10^{-2}\ kg}

    \mathrm{625\ \mu s = 6.25\times 10^2\ \bcancel{\mu s} \color{blue}{\frac{10^{-6}\ s}{1\ \bcancel{\mu s}}} = 6.25\times 10^{-4}\ s}

    \mathrm{6.40\ cm^3 = 6.40\ \bcancel{cm^3} \color{blue}{\left(\frac{10^{-2}\ m}{1\ \bcancel{cm}}\right)^3} = 6.40\times 10^{-6}\ m^3}

    \mathrm{80.0\ \frac{km}{h} = 80.0\ \frac{\bcancel{km}}{\bcancel{h}} \color{blue}{\cdot\frac{10^3\ m}{1\ \bcancel{km}} \cdot \frac{1\ \bcancel{h}}{3600\ s}} = 22.2\ m/s}

  2. Convert these measurements to the specified unit.
    1. 0.0718 kg [convert to grams]
    2. 0.925 mA [convert to μA]
    3. 8.5 m/s [convert to km/h]
    4. 3.52 g/cm3 [convert to kg/m3]

    3. \mathrm{0.0718\ kg = 7.18\times 10^{-2}\ \bcancel{kg} \color{blue}{\cdot\frac{10^3\ g}{1\ \bcancel{kg}}} = 71.8\ g}

    \mathrm{0.925\ mA = 9.25\times 10^{-1}\ \bcancel{mA} \color{blue}{\cdot\frac{10^{-3} \bcancel{A}}{1\ \bcancel{mA}}\cdot \frac{1\ \mu A}{10^{-6}\ \bcancel{A}}} = 925\ \mu A}

    \mathrm{8.5\ m/s = 8.5\ \frac{\bcancel{m}}{\bcancel{s}} \color{blue}{\cdot\frac{1\ km}{10^3\ \bcancel{m}} \cdot\frac{3600\ \bcancel{s}}{1\ h}} = 31\ km/h}

    \mathrm{3.52\ g/cm^3 = 3.52\ \frac{\bcancel{g}}{\bcancel{cm^3}} \color{blue}{\cdot\frac{1\ kg}{10^3\ \bcancel{g}} \left(\frac{\bcancel{cm}}{10^{-2}\ m}\right)^3} = 3.52\times 10^3\ kg/m^3}

  3. Earth’s atmosphere has a “standard” air pressure of 14.7 psi (pounds per square inch). Express this measurement using the standard SI unit, N/m2 (also called pascals, Pa). The conversion factors are 1 inch = 0.0254 m and 1 pound = 4.45 newtons.

    4. \mathrm{14.7\ \frac{lb}{in^2} = 14.7\ \frac{\bcancel{lb}}{\bcancel{in}^2} \color{blue}{\cdot \frac{4.45\ N}{1\ \bcancel{lb}} \left(\frac{1\ \bcancel{in}}{0.0254\ m}\right)^2} = 1.01\times 10^5\ Pa}
