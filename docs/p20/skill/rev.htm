Scientific Notation

Numbers can be written in decimal notation:

\rm 149\,600\,000\,000\ m

..or scientific notation:

\rm 1.496\times 10^{11}\ m

The exponent in the power of 10 indicates how many positions to move the decimal point when changing between decimal and scientific notation.

  • In scientific notation, the decimal point is always positioned immediately after the first non-zero digit of the number.
  • Numbers smaller than 1 have a negative exponent when expressed in scientific notation.
  • Numbers equal to or larger than 10 have a positive exponent when expressed in scientific notation.

SI Units

The fundamental units of the Système Internationale include metres, amperes, seconds, kilograms, and kelvins.

  • Fundamental units have an arbitrary defintion.
  • Derived units, such as m/s, are based on combining fundamental units.
  • Some derived units are given their own name: newton, joule, watt, pascal, etc.
  • Metric prefixes (e.g. kilo, centi, micro) are used to make larger or smaller units.

To convert units using the unit factor method...

  • Decide how many of each unit you are converting to equal how many of the units you are converting from. You may need to refer to your Physics 20 Data Sheet if you have not memorized all of the prefixes.

    \rm \color{red} 1\ kg = 10^3\ g

    \rm \color{blue} 1\ cm = 10^{-2}\ m

  • Multiply your measurement by a fraction with the equivalent units on the top and bottom:

    \rm 5.0\ \frac{g}{cm^3} {\color{red}\left(\frac{1\ kg}{10^3\ g}\right) \color{blue}\left(\frac{1\ cm}{10^{-2}\ m}\right)^3} = 5.0\times 10^3\ \frac{kg}{m^3}

  • Make sure that the original units cancel by placing them in the opposite location (numerator and denominator) compared to the original units.
  • If the original unit is squared or cubed, you must use the same exponent with the unit conversion fraction.

Algebra

When using an equation to solve for an unknown variable, you must first isolate the unknown.

  • Each operation that involves the unknown must be cancelled by performing the inverse operation. E.g. division cancels multiplication, squaring cancels square root, \sin^{-1} cancels \sin.
  • You must perform the same operation on both sides of the equation.
  • Order of operations matters! When you have more than one operation to cancel, proceed in the reverse order of operations, since cancelling only works when the inverse operations are consecutive.

Trigonometry

For all triangles:

\angle A + \angle B + \angle C = 180^\circ

When a triangle contains a right angle, we can solve for the missing sides or angles using the following additional relationships:

\rm Hyp^2 = Opp^2 + Adj^2

\sin\theta = \rm \frac{Opp}{Hyp}

\cos\theta = \rm \frac{Adj}{Hyp}

\tan\theta = \rm \frac{Opp}{Adj}

  • When solving for an angle, you must use \sin^{-1}, \cos^{-1}, or \tan^{-1}, which are the inverse operations of \sin, \cos, and \tan.
  • In Physics 20, you will want to make sure your calculator is set to “Degree” mode. (You will use “Radian” mode in Math 30-1 and Math 31.)
