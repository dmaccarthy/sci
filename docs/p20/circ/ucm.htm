Uniform Circular Motion

Uniform Circular Motion

Uniform circular motion is motion along a circular trajectory with a constant speed.

  • Because the direction of motion is always changing, the velocity is not constant.

For motion along a circle, several new terms are commonly used to describe directions:

  • Radial (or “radially outward”) means the direction pointing directly away from the circle’s centre, at any point on the circumference.
  • Centripetal (or “radially inward”) means the direction pointing directly toward from the circle’s centre, at any point on the circumference.
  • Tangential means the along the circle’s circumference, perpendicular to the centripetal and radial directions.

Dynamics of UCM

According to Newton’s Laws, an unbalanced force is required to change the direction of the velocity vector.

  • For uniform circular motion, the net force will always be in the centripetal direction.
  • For example, Earth’s (nearly) circular orbit is caused by the Sun’s gravitational attraction.
  • When scientists talk about “centripetal force”, they are only referring to the direction of the net force, not to the type of force.
  • Centripetal force can be gravity (as in the Earth’s orbit), electric (as in the orbit of an electron in an atom), friction (as in a car rounding a curve), or any other type (or combination of types!) of force.

UCM Relations

The time that it takes an object to complete one full circular orbit is called the period.

  • As for SHM, the symbol for period is a capital T, to distinguish it from t, which is the time coordinate.
  • Frequency is the reciprocal of period:

f=\frac{1}{T}

  • While period measures the time per cycle or obit, frequency measures the number of cycles per second.
  • The SI unit of frequency is hertz (Hz = s–1).
  • Frequency is sometimes measured in “rpm” (rotations per minute). To convert rpm to Hz, divide by 60 since Hz means rotations per second.
  • Since the distance travelled each orbit is equal to the circumference of the circular orbit, 2\pi r, the period and the speed of the motion are related by the equation:

v=\frac{2\pi r}{T}

  • Note the similarity to the SHM equation:

v_{max}=\frac{2\pi A}{T}

Example 1

A student stands on a carousel at a distance of 2.00 metres from the centre. Another student times the period of the motion to be 8.50 seconds. Calculate the speed of the student.

v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}

v = \rm \frac{2\pi\cdot 2.00\ m}{8.50\ s} = 1.48\ m/s

Example 2

A car tire has a radius of 44.0 cm. Calculate the frequency and period of the rotation when the car is moving at 80.0 km/h.

v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}

vT = 2\pi r

T = \frac{2\pi r}{v}

T = \rm \frac{2\pi\cdot 0.440\ m}{22.2\ m/s} = 0.124\ s

f = \frac{1}{T} = \rm{8.04\ Hz}

Practice

  1. Earth’s orbit around the Sun is very nearly circular. The period of Earth’s orbit is 365.24 days, and the orbital radius is 1.496 × 1011 m.
    1. Which force acts as the centripetal force for Earth’s motion?

      2. The Sun’s gravity acting on the Earth is the centripetal force.

    2. Calculate Earth’s orbital speed.

      3. v = \frac{2\pi r}{T} = \rm \frac{2\pi \cdot 1.496\times 10^{11}\ m}{3.156\times 10^7\ s} = 2.979\times 10^4\ m/s

  2. A cyclist takes 15.2 seconds to complete one lap of a circular track. The bike is equipped with a speedometer that indicates the speed is 32.0 km/h.
    1. Identify the force that causes the bike to move in circular motion.

      2. Friction between the tires and the ground. This is the only unbalanced force because the normal force balances gravity on a flat surface.

    2. Calculate the radius of the track.

      3. v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}

      vT = 2\pi r

      r = \frac{vT}{2\pi} = {\rm \frac{8.89\ m/s\cdot 15.2\ s}{2\pi} = 21.5\ m}

  3. An electric generator contains a coil of copper wire that moves in a circle with r = 25.0 cm. The generator rotates with a frequency of 3000 rpm (rotations per minute).
    1. Convert the frequency to the standard unit (hertz) and use the result to calculate the period of rotation.

      2. f = \rm 3000\ \frac{rotations}{minute} = \frac{3000\ rotations}{60\ s} = 50.0\ Hz

      T = \frac{1}{f} = \rm \frac{1}{50.0\ Hz} = 0.0200 s

    2. Determine the speed of the coil.

      3. v = \frac{2\pi r}{T} = \rm \frac{2\pi\cdot 0.250\ m}{0.0200\ s} = 78.5\ m/s

  4. A satellite in uniform circular motion around the Earth has an orbital radius of 1.50 × 107 m and orbits with a speed of 5.16 km/s. How long does it take the satellite to make one complete orbit of Earth?

    5. v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}

    vT = 2\pi r

    T = \frac{2\pi r}{v} = \rm \frac{2\pi\cdot 1.50\times 10^7\ m}{5160\ m/s} = 1.83\times 10^4\ s = 5.07\ h

Review

    1. Define uniform circular motion.

      2. Motion in a circular path with constant speed.

    2. Define: (i) radial, (ii) centripetal, (iii) tangential.

      3. Radial is the direction directly away from the centre. Centripetal is toward the centre. Tangential means along the circumference, perpendiuclar to the radial and centripetal directions.

    1. Using Newton’s Laws, explain why uniform circular motion can only occur when there is an unbalanced force.

      2. If the unbalanced force was zero, the direction of the velocity (as well as the magnitude) would be constant; the motion would be a straight line.

    2. What is the direction of the net force for UCM?

      3. Centripetal

    3. Explain how the speed of an object can be constant even though there is an unbalanced force.

      4. The direction of the velocity is changing due to the force. Because the force acts perpendicular to the motion, it does zero work, so the speed remains constant.

    1. Define the period of UCM.

      2. The time for one complete circle.

    2. Define frequency and explain how it relates to period.

      3. Frequency (cycles per second) is the reciprocal of period (seconds per cycle).

    3. Define the SI unit of frequency. Explain how to convert between “rpm” and the standard unit.

      4. Frequency is measured in hertz:

      \rm Hz = s^{-1}

      To convert rpm to Hz, divide by 60:

      \rm 1\ Hz = 60\ rpm

  4. Write and explain the equation for the relationship between period, radius, and speed of UCM.

    5. v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}
