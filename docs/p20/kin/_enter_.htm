Acceleration

  1. A car accelerates from 50.0 km/h to 80.0 km/h in 4.00 seconds. Calculate the carâ€™s acceleration. [Important: km/h is not the standard SI unit of velocity!]

    2. Given:

    {\vec{\bf v}}_i = \rm{50.0\ km/h = 50.0 \frac{1000\ m}{3600\ s} = 13.889\ m/s}

    {\vec{\bf v}}_f = \rm{80.0\ km/h = 80.0 \frac{1000\ m}{3600\ s} = 22.222\ m/s}

    \Delta t = \rm{4.00\ s}

    Solution:

    \vec{\bf a} = \frac{\Delta{\vec{\bf v}}}{\Delta t} = \rm{\frac{22.222\ m/s - 13.889\ m/s}{4.00\ s} = +2.08\ m/s^2}

  2. A soccer ball kicked straight up reaches its highest point in 2.50 seconds. Calculate the velocity with which the ball was kicked. Neglect the effects of air resistance.

    3. Given / Assumed:

    \Delta t = \rm 2.50\ s

    \vec{\bf v}_f = \rm 0.00\ m/s

    \vec{\bf a} = \rm -9.81\ m/s^2

    Isolate the initial velocity:

    \vec{\bf a} = \frac{\vec{\bf v}_f - \vec{\bf v}_i}{\Delta t}

    \vec{\bf a} \Delta t = \vec{\bf v}_f - \vec{\bf v}_i

    \vec{\bf a} \Delta t + \vec{\bf v}_i = \vec{\bf v}_f

    \vec{\bf v}_i = \vec{\bf v}_f - \vec{\bf a} \Delta t

    Evaluate:

    \vec{\bf v}_i = \rm 0.00\ m/s - (-9.81\ m/s^2)\ 2.50\ s = +24.5\ m/s
