Computing Science 10: Tests & Mini-Projects

All programs must be written in replit using the same CS10 repl (shared with your teacher!) that you use for your daily assignments. Do not paste programs from the internet or from another IDE!

Programs will be assessed for whether or not they meet the requirements of the task, as well as for the following additional criteria:

  • use comments and docstrings where appropriate
  • use descriptive variable names
  • use “Input-Processing-Output” (IPO) program structure where appropriate
  • avoid “bad” programming practices like the break and continue statements
  • make interactive programs user-friendly
  • do not use features that are more advanced than what you have learned in the lesson notes
